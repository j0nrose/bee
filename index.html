Native Bee Species

California Carpenter Bee

Scientific Name: Xylocopa californica
Size: 20-25mm
Flight Season: March - October
Habitat: Coastal areas, gardens

Large, robust bees with shiny black bodies. Females excavate tunnels in wood for nesting. Important pollinators of open-faced flowers and excellent for native plant gardens.

Blue Orchard Mason Bee

Scientific Name: Osmia lignaria
Size: 12-14mm
Flight Season: March - June
Habitat: Orchards, woodlands

Metallic blue-black solitary bees that nest in hollow stems and holes. Extremely efficient pollinators of fruit trees, working even in cool weather when honeybees remain inactive.

Alfalfa Leafcutter Bee

Scientific Name: Megachile rotundata
Size: 8-12mm
Flight Season: June - September
Habitat: Agricultural areas, gardens

Medium-sized bees that cut circular pieces from leaves to construct their nests. Essential for alfalfa seed production and highly efficient at pollinating legumes.

California Sweat Bee

Scientific Name: Halictus tripartitus
Size: 6-10mm
Flight Season: April - October
Habitat: Gardens, meadows, urban areas

Small, metallic green bees that nest in soil. Highly social with complex colony structures. Important pollinators of wildflowers and crops, attracted to human perspiration.

Yellow-faced Bumble Bee

Scientific Name: Bombus vosnesenskii
Size: 15-20mm
Flight Season: February - November
Habitat: Coastal scrub, gardens

Robust social bees with distinctive yellow faces and fuzzy bodies. Form colonies with up to 200 workers. Excellent buzz pollinators for tomatoes and native California plants.

Wool Carder Bee

Scientific Name: Anthidium manicatum
Size: 11-17mm
Flight Season: May - September
Habitat: Gardens, urban areas

Medium-sized bees that collect plant fibers to line their nests. Males are territorial and defend flowers aggressively. Excellent pollinators of mint family plants and garden flowers.

Conservation Efforts

Habitat Restoration

Creating and maintaining native plant gardens that provide year-round blooms and nesting sites for California's diverse bee populations.

Pesticide Reduction

Promoting integrated pest management and organic farming practices to reduce harmful chemical exposure to pollinators.

Research & Monitoring

Conducting ongoing studies to track bee population trends and identify conservation priorities across California ecosystems.

Community Education

Teaching the public about native bee identification, habitat creation, and the importance of pollinator conservation.
